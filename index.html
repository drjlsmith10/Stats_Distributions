Bernoulli Distribution
DISCRETE Single yes/no trial
Real World: A single coin flip, one free throw attempt, whether one email is spam or not
Parameters
p
"p"
Probability of Success
The likelihood that a single trial results in a "success" (the outcome you're counting). Must be between 0 and 1.
Directly determines both the mean (E[X] = p) and the shape of the distribution. Higher p shifts probability mass toward 1.
q
"q"
Probability of Failure
The complement of p. Always equals (1 - p). Sometimes written explicitly in formulas.
q = 1 - p. Used in variance calculation: Var(X) = p(1-p) = pq

E[X] = p
Variance
Var(X) = p(1-p)
Outcomes
X ‚àà {0, 1}
The building block of all discrete distributions. Only two outcomes: success (1) or failure (0).

CONTINUOUS Equal probability everywhere
Real World: Random number generators, rounding errors, arrival time within a fixed window, spinning a fair spinner
Parameters
a
"a"
Minimum (Lower Bound)
The smallest possible value. No outcomes can occur below this point. Can be any real number.

The largest possible value. No outcomes can occur above this point. Must be greater than a (b > a).
Sets the right edge. The height of the PDF = 1/(b-a), ensuring total area = 1. Wider range = lower height.

The "maximum ignorance" distribution. When you only know the range but have no reason to favor any value. Also called rectangular distribution.
